The results, which reflect a deeply divided electorate, revealed Trump winning 30 states while Harris secured 20, with one state, Maine, split between the two candidates.

The election saw unprecedented voter turnout, with various states reporting record numbers of early and mail-in ballots. In Georgia, Secretary of State Brad Raffensperger remarked, "As the 2024 presidential election heads into its final days, all signs in Georgia point to a tight race." This sentiment was echoed across battleground states, each reflecting a deepening polarization in American politics.

Despite a significant push from both campaigns, it was Trump's narrative of being "the candidate for change" that resonated most strongly with voters in key demographics, particularly among working-class citizens disillusioned with traditional political rhetoric.

As the election results rolled in, Harris expressed her gratitude for the support she received, vowing to continue fighting for the values she believes in. "This campaign has always been about the people, and I will never stop advocating for what is right," she stated in her concession speech.

The aftermath of the election is expected to be contentious, with both parties already planning their strategies for the upcoming midterms, as well as future presidential races.
